Procedural Flowers

Introduction

To explore my fascination of procedural generation I’ve created a small tool to procedurally generate flowers in order to be able to create flowers from scratch. In order to realise this project I used Unity 2021.3.15 to visualise the scripts I wrote in C#. In it’s current form, this tool is executed in editor mode in Unity where you can adjust the different settings of the flower petals and the overall flower.

Flower Petals

To create the flower petals I studied the L-systems used in The Algorithmic Beauty of Plants to generate models of plant organs. There I encountered the following figure that, along with Vincent Taylor’s approach, inspired the way I shaped the flower petals.
After studying the L-systems used I decided that instead of transcribing the L-system to work within C#, I would implement the steps in a similar, but for me more familiar way.
In this approach I created nodes to represent the vertices needed to generate the shape. These nodes are then used to triangulate the shape to generate the mesh. By manipulating different settings, the distribution of the nodes can vary and result in different shapes of petals.

Flower Head

To distribute the flower petals on the flower head I’ve used Phyllotaxis, a mathematical phenomenon which is found in nature.
Using this placement, the individual petals are then placed on the flower head and rotated to face inwards towards the center of the flower head. Using the settings of the flower head, the petals can be further rotated to create a blooming effect.

Color

Finally I colored the flower petals using a gradient, which colors each individual petal a different color depending on it’s position on the flower head.
